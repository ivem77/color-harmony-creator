Analogous Harmony

These three neighboring colors on the color wheel work together naturally. The middle color leads the composition, while its neighbors add depth and interest. Like watching colors shift in a sunset, analogous colors create visual flow that feels effortless and harmonious.

Complementary Harmony

Meet the power couple of color theory. Sitting opposite each other on the color wheel, these two colors create instant chemistry through maximum contrast. Each color makes its partner appear more vibrant and alive.

Triadic Harmony

Spaced evenly at 120 degrees around the color wheel, three colors unite to create balanced energy. Like a perfect triangle, triadic colors offer stability with a creative twist. This harmony mixes warm and cool tones while maintaining clear relationships between colors.

Split-Complementary Harmony

Take a base color, look across the wheel to its complement, then step one color to each side – that's split-complementary. This arrangement delivers the punch of complementary colors but feels more sophisticated and flexible.

Tetradic Harmony

Also known as double complementary, this scheme uses four colors arranged into two complementary pairs. Like a rectangle on the color wheel, tetradic harmony offers rich, dynamic possibilities while maintaining balance through symmetry. Perfect for designs that need variety and sophistication.

Monochromatic Harmony

One color tells many stories through its range of light and dark values. Monochromatic schemes explore a single hue's full potential – from its boldest form to its subtlest whisper. By playing with saturation and brightness, this harmony creates depth while maintaining perfect unity.
